Parish News

SALE OF ST NICHOLAS’ CHURCH

As I am sure you are all aware we have been trying to sell St Nicholas’s church so we can pay off our debts and to carry out work to our church hall above the sacristy. However, the process has been very slow and we have made little progress on the sale. Though there have already been many meetings between parishioners and the previous priests and parish councils (all the parishioners unanimously agreeing to sell St Nicholas church asap), we held another Parish Council Meeting and a Finance Committee Meeting to discuss our options and have decided to put the sale of the church on the open market. Also, we wish to keep the house attached to the property to develop and turn it into two flats which will give the parish a much-needed boost to our income. All of this has been discussed before and has been agreed by the parishioners. We voted and unanimously agreed to put the church on the open market and to retain the house to develop into flats. If there are no objections from any parishioners, then we would like to put the proposals to the diocese for consideration. Fr Tony Grant.

ALTAR SERVERS’ FORMATION CELEBRATION DAY

25th OCTOBER AT ST ANTONY’S CHURCH: There will be Altar Servers from all the deanery parishes who will meet at St Antony’s church on Saturday 25th October 9.30 am4.30 pm. Community

FOOD DISTRIBUTION TO THE HOMELESS

The Coptics at St Nicholas’ church distribute sandwiches and food to the homeless each Monday at Manor Park Library 8.15pm-10.00pm. They start at St Mary and St George Coptic Orthodox Church, aka St Nicholas church, 21 Gladding Road E12 5DD from 7:00-8:00pm for food preparation and distribute the food from 8:15-10:00pm in front of Manor Park Library. They give sandwiches, fruits, crisps, cupcakes, hot soup, and hot drinks. They also sometimes give clothes and some toiletries. If you would like to volunteer to help them or donate food, etc., then please contact us by telephone Fr Tony (0208- 478-1895) or by emailing Fr Tony at: manorpark@brcdt.org. Or contact Halim, the Copts’ secretary at: halim.salama@aon.co.uk. To find when you can help or deliver food to St Nicholas’ church.
